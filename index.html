Abstract

This essay explores the success of Black Myth: Wukong as an adaptation of the classic Chinese novel, Journey to the West by Chengen Wu. The Effect and Challenge of Digital Adaption on Classic Literary Works--80 Days by Jules Gabriel Verne

Posted on 2024-11-02

Abstract

This essay explores the effects and challenges of digital adaptation on classic literary works, focusing specifically on Jules Verne’s classic novel”80 Days.” With emerging digital technologies and the popularity of internet, classic novels can now reach broader audiences through various media formats like movies, games, and digital adaptations. The essay discusses how these adaptations make literature accessible to new generations and how the process reshapes the engagement with these timeless stories. On the other hand, adaptations to these novels also face significant challenges, such as retaining the depth and essence of the original work and overcoming issues like intellectual isolation in digital platforms. As an example, this essay proposes a board game adaptation of “80 Days” and discussed about how these adaptions will affect the original essay and attract the Z-generation. The adaptation aims to balance innovation with respect for the original artistic vision, making the classic works be prepared for attracting modern audiences.
